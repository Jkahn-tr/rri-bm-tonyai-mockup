Tony AI
Last week you said Sarah ran her first three calls solo. How did they go?
Two were good. One she talked too long and lost the close.
What was the moment she should have stopped and asked for the next step?
When the customer stopped asking about price and started asking about availability. She kept selling anyway.
That's a trainable trigger. One sentence: "When they ask about availability, stop selling and confirm the next step." Can you give that to her in writing today?
Yeah. I can do that this afternoon.
Done. I'll hold that. You said $180K/year hinges on this. That one sentence might be the whole thing.

Three Posture Rules
1. Open with their words, never the clock. The attendee never hears "it's week 3" — only their own intention reflected back.
2. Always offer, never assign. "Want to?" not "Time to." Saying "not now" earns no guilt.
3. Go quiet when ignored. Two unanswered invitations become silence — not a third louder one.
