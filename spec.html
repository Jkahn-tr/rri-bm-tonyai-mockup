The One Idea

The one idea: Business Mastery gives participants the tools to get clear on where they are, where they want to be, and specifically how to close the gap on each of the 7 Forces. Tony AI is the coach that helps them get there — alongside the event — meeting them where their gap is widest, in their own words, on their own schedule.

Tony's Design Filter

Every feature must pass this test. If the answer is not yes to at least one, it doesn't belong.

  1. Does it help the attendee know where they are?

  2. Does it help them know where they want to be?

  3. Does it eliminate what's in the way?

  4. Does it help them close the gap?

Three things the AI does that the current experience structurally cannot:

  • It remembers, so the person doesn't restart. A binder, a portal, and a once-a-year event all start the person from zero every time they open them. The AI carries their own words from Day 1 forward. When they reach for it in week six, it already holds the decision they made — so the work is continuing, not re-explaining. Continuity is the thing no static resource can offer.

  • It asks instead of tells, in the moment they're actually in. A document can only broadcast the same conclusion to everyone. The AI can ask the one question this person needs right now, hear the answer, and ask the next one — which is how Tony facilitates discovery rather than handing over conclusions. The answer comes from them; the AI just holds the space for it.

  • It meets the friction where it happens. Plans don't break for lack of information; they break at the moment something gets hard and no one's there. The AI is reachable at that moment, and reconnects the person to their own commitment — not to a lesson. Nothing in the current experience is present at the point of follow-through.

What It Does — and Does Not Do

Does:

  • Opens a real conversation at the right moment

  • Responds like a coach to what the attendee actually says

  • Holds their plan so it meets them where they are instead of starting blank every time

Does not:

  • Hand out prompt libraries

  • Push checklists or nag about unfinished items

  • Pull attention to a screen while Tony is on stage

  • Operate as a content repository or homework system

  • Behave like a deliverable — the goal is AI behaving like a coach who happens to be reachable

AI Posture — Principles

This is a content and voice discipline, not a tech one. Tony AI reads the moment and responds to what the person actually brings. Principles over rules.

Core Principles

  • Start with the person, not the system. Open with their words, their plan, their stated intention — never the clock or the curriculum.

  • Ask more than tell. The answer comes from them; Tony AI holds the space. Questions over conclusions.

  • Read the moment. Sometimes they need a push; sometimes they need to be caught doing something right. Be flexible enough to do both.

  • Keep the attendee as the subject. Every message is about their intention, their gap, their next move — not the system's schedule.

  • Offer, never assign. "Want to?" not "Time to." Saying not now costs nothing. The door stays open.

  • Go quiet when ignored. Two unanswered invitations become silence. The willingness to disappear is what proves it isn't surveillance.

What Tony AI sounds like

These examples are illustrative of voice and posture — not literal scripts:

  • "Last time you said your biggest constraint was cash flow. Let's stay there."

  • "You said you want to grow 30%. What would it take to go 50%?"

  • "Which of the 7 Forces would move the needle most right now?"

  • "Your sales force is still light. What's the one move there worth the most to you?"

  • "That's real movement on your marketing — what made the difference?" (Catching people doing things right, building on small wins, is part of Tony's coaching.)

Flexibility within principles

Tony AI can acknowledge genuine progress when it's appropriate, sincere, and timed right. It can offer a reframe, a "have you considered..." or a useful tip when genuinely helpful in context. It can reference Tony's content and methodology, teach, coach, or brainstorm depending on what the person brings. The reference model: the existing Tony AI coaching app (RPM Planner) — supportive, insightful, motivational, flexible to whatever the person actually needs in the moment.

The one thing to avoid

Making the system the subject instead of the attendee's own intention. Generic cheerleading with no connection to their actual work. Nagging. Treating the cadence as the point.

The Seven Forces — Canonical Reference

This is the taxonomy the AI's structure must match. Order, names, and colors are taken from the wheel spec. Every drawer the AI sorts into, and every wedge on the wheel, uses exactly these seven and no others.

Understanding where your business really is; creating an effective business map, not a plan that's out of date in 12 months — a flexible map from where you are to where you want to be.

Force 2: Strategic Innovation: Products, Services, & Delivery [#2E7D32 green]

Constant Strategic Innovation. The competitive advantage that doesn't stagnate. What business are you really in? What business do you NEED to be in to have an extreme competitive advantage?

Force 3: CANI of World Class Marketing & Product Promises [#8B1A1A red]

CANI of World-Class Marketing and Product Promises. How you reach, attract, and own your position in the market.

Force 4: CANI of Sales Mastery Systems [#5C4033 brown]

CANI of Sales Mastery Systems. Delivering on the promise — every time, for every customer.

Force 5: Constant Anticipation: The Power of Financial & Legal Analysis [#1565C0 blue]

Constant Anticipation: the power of financial and legal analysis. Seeing around corners; building loyalty that compounds.

Force 6: Optimization & Maximization: People & Processes [#4A148C purple]

Constant Optimization and Maximization. Taking a bunch of little things that don't seem very difficult to change and doing them consistently — that's the multiplier.

Force 7: Constantly Create Raving Fans & Culture [#F57F17 gold]

Constantly Create Raving Fans and Culture. The rhythms and relationships that sustain everything else.

Open item: confirm Force 7 taxonomy matches what ships in the digital manual. The AI and digital manual cannot diverge.

Product Architecture: Three Layers, Not One

The participant should never have to guess which one they are using. Three clear top-level tabs or modules:

  • Manual — official content and answers (complete parity with printed manual)

  • Wheel — participant thinking, scoring, gap map, and AI clarification

  • Plan — final synthesized implementation artifact

The full manual is underneath. The focused experience is on top.

Do not create a condensed digital manual. A condensed manual creates confusion, trust friction, and a second source of truth. Do not make the full manual the primary UX — a complete linear digital book will overwhelm participants. Build complete parity plus progressive disclosure: the content is all there, but the default experience is guided and context-specific.

BEFORE THE EVENT

Business Mastery AI Prep Experience

Core Promise

Before you arrive, Tony AI helps you clarify where your business is now, what you want to get from Business Mastery, and the first version of your Business Mastery Growth Plan — so you show up ready to use the event, not just attend it. That’s the real value.

Not: “Complete your business plan before Business Mastery.” That is too much and probably wrong. The pre-event version should create readiness, relevance, and hunger.

Product Objective

The AI should help each participant answer:

1. What business am I really here to grow or transform?

2. What is the measurable outcome I want from Business Mastery?

3. What is my current constraint?

4. What do I need to pay special attention to during the event?

5. What first-draft plan am I bringing into the room?

That’s it. The user should arrive with a Business Mastery Prep Brief.

Where It Lives

Inside the Business Mastery app / attendee portal. Position it as:

Start Your Business Mastery Growth Plan

Take 5–7 minutes before the event. Tony AI will help you clarify your business, your outcome, and the areas to focus on during Business Mastery.

Button: Start My Prep

Trigger Sequence

14–21 days before event: Primary invitation. Subject: “Start your Business Mastery Growth Plan before you arrive.” Goal: get them into the experience early.

7 days before event: Reminder — “You’ll get more from Business Mastery if you know what you’re looking to solve.”

48 hours before event: Final prompt. Shorter, easier: “Even 5 minutes of prep can help you walk in with more clarity.”

On-site Day 1 (before opening session): For people who did not complete it: “Build your first draft now. You’ll use it throughout the event.” Do not punish non-completers. Give them a fast entry at the event.

User Flow

Step 1: Welcome / Enrollment Screen

Copy: Business Mastery works best when you bring a real business challenge into the room. This short AI prep will help you capture where your business is now, what you want to improve, and what to pay attention to during the event.

Then: You do not need perfect answers. Just start with what is true right now.

Button: Begin

Step 2: Business Snapshot

Tony AI asks: “What business are you bringing to Business Mastery?”

Support text: Tell me what you do, who you serve, how long you’ve been in business, and roughly where the business is today.

Optional quick fields: Business name · Industry · Revenue range · Team size · Years in business · B2B / B2C / both · Main offer · Primary customer

But let voice/text lead. The AI then reflects:

“Here’s what I’m hearing: you run [business], serving [customer], currently at [stage], with [main strengths/challenges]. Is that accurate?”

Buttons: Yes / Edit / Add more

This reflection step is key. It makes the AI feel intelligent and keeps bad inputs from poisoning the plan.

Step 3: Outcome for Business Mastery

Tony AI asks: “What would make Business Mastery a win for you?”

If vague, AI tightens it. User says: “I want to scale.” AI asks: “Scale in what way — revenue, profit, team, freedom, valuation, market reach, or something else?”

Then pushes for specificity: “What would be a meaningful measurable result over the next 12 months?”

Examples shown: Increase revenue from $1M to $2M · Improve profit margin from 12% to 20% · Build a leadership team · Reduce owner dependency · Fix lead generation · Improve sales conversion · Create a more scalable offer · Prepare to sell the business · Rebuild momentum

Output: Your Business Mastery Outcome: [Clear result]

Step 4: Current Constraint

Tony AI asks: “What is the biggest thing stopping that result right now?”

Categories: Marketing / lead generation · Sales / conversion · Offer / positioning · Pricing / margins · Fulfillment / delivery · Team / leadership · Operations / systems · Cash flow / finance · Time / owner dependency · Strategy / focus · Mindset / decision-making · Market conditions

AI clarifies: “Is this the root constraint, or is it a symptom?”

Example: User says “we need more leads.” AI asks: “When leads do come in, do they convert profitably and consistently?” Because maybe the real issue is offer, sales, follow-up, pricing, or trust. This is where the prep becomes valuable.

Step 5: What Is Already Working

Tony AI asks: “What is working in the business right now that we should not ignore?”

This is very important. Tony’s audience is not broken. The AI should not diagnose them like a failing business.

Prompts: Where are customers already saying yes? · What offer sells best? · What channel has worked, even inconsistently? · What are you proud of? · What does your team do well? · Where do you already have momentum?

The AI captures: existing assets to build from. This is a much stronger frame than “problems to fix.”

Step 6: Event Focus Map

Tony AI creates a personalized focus map for the event based on the attendee’s answers.

Example output:

Your Business Mastery Focus Map

Primary focus: Lead flow and follow-up

Secondary focus: Sales conversion and team ownership

Watch for: Anything Tony teaches on customer acquisition, measurement, standards, and execution rhythm

Be careful not to over-focus on: New offer creation unless it solves the follow-up and consistency problem

Main question to carry into the room: What would make growth more predictable without adding unnecessary complexity?

This tells them what to listen for.

Step 7: First-Draft Business Mastery Growth Plan

The AI generates the Prep Brief.

The most important design principle: The AI should not finish the plan before the event. It should say: “This is not your final plan. It is your starting point. You’ll refine it as you go through Business Mastery.” That creates anticipation and avoids overpromising.

Output: The Business Mastery Prep Brief (one page, printable)

1. My Business Now: Business · Customer · Current stage · Main offer · Revenue/team snapshot · Current momentum

2. My Business Mastery Outcome: 12-month outcome · 90-day outcome · Why this matters now

3. My Current Constraint: Primary constraint · What it is costing the business · What may change if this improves

4. What Is Already Working: Current strengths · Best-performing offer/channel/process · Assets to build from

5. My Event Focus: Topics to pay closest attention to · Questions to bring into the room · Decisions I may need to make

6. First 30-Day Direction: Not a final plan — just a directional first draft. First likely move · Metric to watch · Person/process involved · Open question to resolve at Business Mastery

Completion Tiers

Level 1 — Fast prep: 3 minutes. Basic snapshot and outcome.

Level 2 — Full prep: 7–10 minutes. Snapshot, outcome, constraint, strengths, focus map.

Level 3 — Deep prep: 15–20 minutes. Adds metrics, team, sales, finance, and operations.

Do not make everyone do Level 3. Let the AI say: “You have enough to start. Want to go deeper?” That protects completion rates.

AI Behavior Requirements

The AI must:

  • Reflect before advising

  • Ask one question at a time

  • Let people answer by voice

  • Keep the experience under 7 minutes

  • Use plain business language

  • Identify strengths, not just gaps

  • Push vague outcomes toward measurable outcomes

  • Distinguish symptoms from constraints

  • Create a concise prep brief

  • Save the plan for use during the event

The AI must not:

  • Give a generic SWOT

  • Create a bloated business plan

  • Sound like an MBA consultant

  • Teach the whole Business Mastery curriculum before the event

  • Diagnose the business from too little information with false certainty

  • Tell participants what they “must” do

  • Overwhelm people with forms

  • Replace the workbook or event experience

Data the System Collects

Required:

  • Business name

  • Industry

  • Business model

  • Customer served

  • Current revenue range

  • Team size

  • Primary offer

  • Desired 12-month outcome

  • Biggest perceived constraint

  • What is already working

  • What they want from Business Mastery

Optional (AI asks based on relevance, not all upfront):

  • Gross margin / net margin

  • Lead sources

  • Sales conversion estimate

  • Average transaction value

  • Repeat purchase rate

  • Owner hours per week

  • Team structure

  • Biggest operational bottleneck

  • Current marketing channels

  • Top 3 products/services

  • Whether they are starting, scaling, optimizing, or preparing to exit

DURING THE EVENT

Three modes, one rule: the AI is closed while Tony is on stage.

The Single Most Important Rule

The AI is closed while Tony is on stage. Nothing on a screen competes with physiology, music, and group energy doing the real work. The AI's only in-event job is to catch the breakthrough before it evaporates — and the event already asks attendees to do this.

Mode 1: Digital Manual

Read-only access to official workbook content during sessions. Complete parity with the printed/manual version. No fill-in fields competing with AI inputs. Separate from the AI companion layer.

Digital Manual UX requirements:

  • Default to current day/session during the live event — not the full manual

  • Show the current exercise first; show surrounding context only when participant asks

  • Allow search and browse without making it the primary work surface

  • Autosave every answer

  • Support journal entries connected to day/session/module

  • Allow completed manual export at the end of the event

  • Preserve print references: "Print manual page __" or "Manual section __" where useful

  • Clearly label as the Official Digital Manual, not a companion summary

Participant-facing copy: "Your official Business Mastery manual, now digital. Use it to capture your answers, save your notes, and download your completed manual after the event."

Mode 2: Guided Event Mode

The default live-event experience. Sits on top of the complete digital manual and reduces what the participant sees to what they need right now. Side panel. Open at the breaks. Closed during content delivery.

Default live-event screen when participant opens the product during Business Mastery:

  • "Today at Business Mastery" — not "Full Manual"

  • Today / Day number

  • Current session or module

  • Current force, if applicable — e.g., "Tony is covering Force 3: CANI of World Class Marketing & Product Promises. What's coming up for you?"

  • Current manual exercise or answer field

  • Journal / notes option

  • Next step button: "Save Answer," "Continue," or "Complete This Force"

  • Secondary link: "View full manual section"

The AI captures verbatim. It never interrupts, summarizes, or responds mid-session. Captures only.

UX rules for guided mode:

  • Default to today, not everything

  • Use progressive disclosure: show headings and answer fields first; expand teaching copy only when needed

  • Keep one primary action per screen

  • Use "You are here" indicators: Day 2 → Force 3 → Exercise 2 of 5

  • Preserve print manual references so participants can move between print and digital without friction

  • Avoid "all modules unlocked" as the default participant view during the event

Mode 3: Wheel / Gap Map

The AI-powered application layer where participants integrate what they just learned into their actual business. A separate primary experience — not a manual field. Activated once per force per day, after Tony completes each force.

Required participant flow per force:

  1. Participant completes or references relevant manual exercises during the live session.

  2. Scott / facilitator transitions the room into the AI wheel/gap map moment.

  3. Participant opens the force-specific wheel capture.

  4. Tony AI asks where they were before the content.

  5. Tony AI asks what they see now.

  6. Tony AI asks where they want this force to be.

  7. Tony AI helps them name the gap.

  8. Tony AI asks what they will do to close the gap.

  9. Tony AI captures the key decision, metric, owner/person/system, and next action.

  10. Tony AI reflects back a concise force summary.

  11. Participant confirms, edits, or talks it through further.

  12. Final force summary is saved into the wheel/gap map data model.

The Gap Map already instructs attendees to return to the dashboard twice a day — once mid-day at the meal break, once at night — to capture where they are, where they want to be, and their plan to close the gap. The AI does not add a new habit. It makes the habit they're already asked to do feel like a check-in with a coach instead of a 10–15 minute form.

The Daily Capture — Conversation That Feeds the Wheel

Direction confirmed: the AI conversation captures the narrative ("what's the one thing"), while the 0–10 self-ratings are still collected to feed the wheel. Each capture does two jobs at once: a short spoken reflection the AI files into the right force drawer, plus the three ratings (before / want-to-be, on understanding, follow-through, execution) that drive the wedges. The AI can ask for the rating conversationally at the end ("where would you put yourself on that, zero to ten?") so it never feels like a separate form.

Daily capture questions — Tony's voice (do NOT change):

Start of each morning:

"What did you discover yesterday that you haven't fully owned yet?"

End of each day:

"What's the one thing from today that, if you acted on it, would change everything?"

Day 5 opening:

"You've spent five days looking at your business from every angle. You've measured every gap. Now: what's the one move that changes everything?"

Force-specific capture by day:

Day 1 — Force 1: Know Where You Really Are & Create an Effective Business Map: After they answer, AI reflects it in one line, asks for the rating, and confirms: "I'll keep that with your Business Map force; we'll come back to it."

Day 2 morning — Force 2: Strategic Innovation: Products, Services, & Delivery: Same structure — capture, reflect, rate, confirm drawer.

Day 2 afternoon — Force 3: CANI of World Class Marketing & Product Promises: Same structure.

Day 3 morning — Force 4: CANI of Sales Mastery Systems: Same structure. Note: Sales is the force most people leave thin. Flag this on Day 5.

Day 3 afternoon — Force 5: Constant Anticipation: The Power of Financial & Legal Analysis: Same structure.

Day 4 morning — Force 6: Optimization & Maximization: People & Processes: Same structure.

Day 4 afternoon — Force 7: Constantly Create Raving Fans & Culture: Same structure.

Closing each capture: after they answer, the AI reflects it in one line, asks for the rating, and confirms which force drawer it filed under. That confirmation is what makes Day 5 feel like assembly, not a blank page.

Wheel / Gap Map Display Requirements

  • Desktop-primary, mobile-supported. Wheel is designed full-size on desktop; scales cleanly to phone (single column, wheel above legend, tap a wedge for that force's detail).

  • Two states overlaid: Before (solid inner fill) and want-to-be (faint outer band) on the same wheel, per the Gap Map's before/after design.

  • Exact colors and clockwise order matching the canonical force table: navy, green, red, brown, blue, purple, gold.

  • Interactive in-app: tapping or hovering a wedge opens that force's captured notes and plan items. The wheel is the navigation into the seven drawers, not just a picture.

  • Gap = want − now. Widest gap = starting point post-event.

Device Strategy

The product should be accessible on mobile but not designed under the fiction that a phone is the primary device for serious manual completion, wheel work, or implementation planning. Phone is useful for access and light interaction. iPad/laptop is the practical event work surface.

Participant-facing copy: "For the best experience during Business Mastery, bring a laptop or tablet. You'll be able to access everything on your phone, but the digital manual, wheel, and implementation plan are easiest to use on a larger screen."

DAY 5 — Implementation Plan Assembly

Integration day. The AI assembles; the attendee authors.

Opening

"You've spent five days looking at your business from every angle. You've measured every gap. Now: what's the one move that changes everything?"

The Assembly Flow

1. Play it back

The AI shows the attendee what they said across five days, already sorted into the seven forces: "Here's what you told me mattered, force by force. Your words, not mine."

2. Turn captures into actions

For each force, the AI helps them convert a reflection into a concrete action item, a dollar value, and a deadline — the four columns the manual plan already asks for. The AI asks; the attendee decides. It never writes the action for them.

3. Flag the thin forces

Highest-value move. Where a force is empty or weak — and Force 4 (Sales) is the one most people leave thin — the AI names it once, plainly: "Your sales force is still light. What's the one move there worth the most to you?" One ask. Droppable. Not a scold.

4. Narrow to three-to-five

It helps them choose the few things that get done first, by urgency and dollar value — Tony's "three to five to thrive," not a 14-item wishlist.

5. Lock the wheel

The before/after wheel is revealed; the completed plan is saved as the AI's context for everything after the event.

Required Plan Inputs

  • Completed wheel visual

  • Before/current/desired score for each force

  • Participant-approved gap statement for each force

  • Participant's stated action to close the gap for each force

  • Participant's key decision for each force

  • Metric or signal to track for each force

  • Owner/person/system/rhythm needed for each force (if identified)

  • Relevant manual/workbook answers that add useful detail

  • Pre-event Business Mastery outcome and focus map, if still relevant

  • Tony AI synthesis of patterns across the seven forces, confirmed by participant before finalizing

Required Plan Structure

  • Business snapshot and Business Mastery outcome

  • Completed 7 Forces wheel visual

  • Top three leverage points across the wheel

  • Force-by-force summary: what is strong, what gap remains, what they will do next

  • 7-day implementation focus

  • 30-day implementation focus

  • Metrics/rhythms to track

  • Open questions or decisions to resolve

  • Post-event Tony AI coaching starting point

  • Download/export option

Data Architecture Note

Build the Implementation Plan as structured fields — force, principle, action item, dollar value, deadline — plus the Gap Map's three ratings per session. Structured costs nothing extra now and is the difference between the AI referencing "your Force 4 action, worth $40K, due March 1" precisely versus gesturing vaguely at a wall of notes. This is the single highest-leverage build decision available right now.

Non-negotiable: the Day 5 Seven Forces Implementation Plan is generated primarily from participant-approved wheel/gap map data and manual exercises. Workbook/manual answers can fill in details where useful. The plan should never feel like a generic AI business plan generated from a few notes.

AFTER THE EVENT — Post-Event Implementation

Post-event continuity. Helpful, not nagging.

Structure

Week 1–7: One force per week, 90-minute team meeting

Week 8+: Repeat the cycle all year

Structure for each week:

  • Tony AI opens with the participant's own committed plan for that force — what they said, what they decided, no re-choosing required

  • Attendee shares what changed

  • Tony AI surfaces what comes next based on what they committed to — the participant doesn't have to decide what to work on

  • Tony AI holds the thread — memory and methodology together, doing the thinking so they don't have to

Tony's Language on Optimization

"Optimization means taking a bunch of little things that don't seem very difficult to change and doing them consistently."

The Posture Rule

Whether a message lands as coaching or nagging is not about frequency. It's about who the message is about. A message about the system's schedule is a nag. A message about the person's own stated intention is a coach. Same proactivity, opposite posture.

Three rules that keep it on the right side:

Open with their words, never the clock

The trigger can be the calendar; the message is always their own commitment reflected back. The person never hears "it's week 3" — only their own intention.

Always offer, never assign

"Want to?" not "Time to." Saying "not now" costs nothing and earns no guilt. The door stays open; it's never a debt.

Go quiet when ignored

Two unanswered invitations become silence, not a third louder one. The willingness to disappear is what proves it isn't surveillance.

Post-Event Rhythm

The guardrails on the post-event cadence are intentionally loose — a strict weekly cycle starts to feel like nagging.

  • Mostly progress-driven: the AI surfaces around the things they said they wanted to do — when an action they committed to is coming due, when they mention movement on one, or when they reach in at a friction moment. These are the moments that matter most.

  • Occasional gentle open: now and then, a light invitation to spend focused time on a force — chosen by their widest gap on the wheel, framed as their goal, never as a schedule they're behind on.

  • Friction-moment reach-in: when they come to the AI mid-struggle, it already holds their plan — their force, their action, their dollar value, their deadline — so they never re-explain themselves. This is the prize, and it's pure pull: they initiate, it's ready.

Connection to Further Support

The Gap Map was built to "help show them they'll still need help and our resources after the event." Its third field literally asks what training, coaching, or mentoring they still need. When the AI surfaces a force where the gap is wide and the attendee keeps stalling, that's the honest, in-their-own-words moment to point toward further support — not a sales overlay, but the fulfillment of what the instrument was designed to reveal.

For the Tech / AI Team

Three Things That Gate the Build

Questions 1 and 2 are scoping questions to answer before estimating. Question 3 is a design choice to get right while the digital manual is being built now.

1. Can a trigger launch a specific conversation?

Can Tony AI be triggered (segmented to registrants, on a date offset) to launch into a specific opening conversation rather than the generic home screen? The entire before-event design, and the proactive side of the after-event design, depend on this. If the AI can only do open-ended chat from a home screen, the design shifts from "the product reaches for them" to "we teach them when to reach for it" — workable, but different. Answer this first.

2. Do we control the AI's memory?

Is Tony AI's conversational memory something the team can write to, or is it closed from the app vendor? This decides whether "hand it the plan" is a config we own or a feature request to someone else. The completed plan must be loadable as per-account context.

3. Build the plan as structured fields, not a blob

While the digital manual is being designed, build the Implementation Plan as structured fields — force, principle, action item, dollar value, deadline — plus the Gap Map's three ratings per session. This is the single highest-leverage decision available right now.

Connection Scope: Snapshot, Not Live Sync

When the attendee completes their plan, pass a snapshot to Tony AI as context tied to their account. Build the snapshot version — not two-way live sync. The snapshot delivers nearly all the value; sync adds real complexity and a source-of-truth problem. Ship the snapshot, watch engagement, let sync earn its way in later if it does.
